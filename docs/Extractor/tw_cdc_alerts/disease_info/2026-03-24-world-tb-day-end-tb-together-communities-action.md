## 摘要

Taiwan CDC held a World TB Day 2026 press conference at the historic Jingxin Garden, showcasing Taiwan's progress in TB control — a 66% reduction in incidence since 2005 to approximately 25 cases per 100,000 in 2025. The event promoted LTBI screening for high-risk groups and community participation toward the 2035 TB elimination goal.

## 詳細內容

In response to World TB Day on March 24, 2026, under the theme "Yes! We can End TB! Led by countries. Powered by people," Taiwan CDC held a press conference titled "End TB Together: Communities in Action" at Jingxin Garden — a historic site that formerly served as the dormitory complex of the Songshan Sanatorium. The event brought together TB prevention experts and scholars, civil society organizations, and community members.

**Taiwan's TB Control Progress:**
- 2025 TB incidence: approximately 25 cases per 100,000 population
- Cumulative decrease of 66% compared to 73 cases per 100,000 in 2005
- Implementation of advanced diagnostic technologies and shorter treatment regimens
- Progressive expansion of LTBI screening and treatment among high-risk populations

**Key Messages from Speakers:**

Taiwan CDC Director-General Yi-Chun Lo highlighted years of joint efforts across the public health sector, medical institutions, civil society organizations, and government agencies. Taiwan has adopted advanced diagnostic technologies and shorter treatment regimens in line with international standards. LTBI screening and treatment have been progressively expanded among high-risk populations to reduce risk of active TB and prevent transmission.

Mr. Ming-chih Yu, Chairman of the Taiwan Anti-Tuberculosis Association, urged individuals experiencing symptoms such as a cough lasting more than two weeks, chest pain, weight loss, fever, or loss of appetite to seek prompt medical evaluation. He also recommended good daily hygiene practices including proper ventilation, respiratory etiquette, and managing chronic conditions.

Dr. Chen-yuan Wang, Chairman of the Taiwan Society of Tuberculosis and Lung Diseases, explained that LTBI is asymptomatic and non-infectious but may progress to active disease over time. With early treatment and medication adherence, the risk of progression can be reduced by approximately 90%.

**High-Risk Groups for LTBI Screening:**
- Close contacts of TB patients
- Immunocompromised individuals
- Residents of long-term care facilities
- Patients undergoing dialysis
- Patients with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
- Substance users
- Those with poorly controlled diabetes

Taiwan, in collaboration with health bureaus across 22 cities and counties, civil society organizations, and the public, is working toward the goal of eliminating TB by 2035.
