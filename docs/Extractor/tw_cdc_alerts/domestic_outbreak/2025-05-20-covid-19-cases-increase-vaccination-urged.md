## 摘要
Taiwan CDC reported escalating COVID-19 infections with 19,097 outpatient/emergency visits in week 20 (May 11-17). The epidemic is projected to peak in mid-to-late June with 55,000-65,000 weekly visits. Over 90% of severe cases and deaths occurred among individuals not vaccinated with the JN.1 vaccine, highlighting the importance of vaccination.

## 詳細內容

### Current Situation
The Taiwan Centers for Disease Control reported escalating domestic infections as of May 20, 2025. Medical visits for COVID-19 reached 19,097 in week 20 (May 11-17), with projections indicating the epidemic will "reach its peak in mid-to-late June, with the highest number of COVID-19 medical visits in a single week estimated to be 55,000 to 65,000."

### Mortality and Vulnerability
This year has documented 330 severe cases and 47 deaths, predominantly affecting elderly individuals and those with chronic conditions. Notably, "more than 90% of the confirmed and fatal cases were among individuals who were not vaccinated with the JN.1 vaccine."

### Vaccination Progress
As of May 18, approximately 2.116 million people received JN.1 vaccination, representing an 8.79% national rate. Among seniors over 65, first-dose coverage stands at 18.3% with second-dose rates at 0.48%. Weekly vaccination appointments jumped 57% in the week of May 12-18.

### Medical Supplies
Taiwan has secured substantial antiviral reserves: 207,000 doses of remdesivir, 469,000 courses of Paxlovid, and 11,000 courses of molnupiravir. Additionally, 3.169 million JN.1 vaccines remain available for persons aged six months and older.
