## 摘要

Taiwan CDC announced on June 7, 2026, that a New Zealand passenger who entered Taiwan on May 7 after traveling aboard Dutch cruise ship MV Hondius completed a 42-day enhanced self-health management period. All four rounds of testing returned negative results for Andes hantavirus. Enhanced monitoring was lifted on June 7, and Taiwan's IHR National Focal Point notified both WHO and New Zealand health authorities.

## 詳細內容

**Passenger Background:**
A New Zealand national arrived in Taiwan on May 7, 2026, following travel aboard MV Hondius, a Dutch cruise ship associated with a cluster of Andes hantavirus cases. Based on expert recommendations, Taiwan CDC placed the passenger under enhanced self-health management in a single-occupancy hospital room for the monitoring period.

**Testing Results:**
The passenger underwent four rounds of testing:
- Round 1: May 14, 2026
- Round 2: May 20, 2026
- Round 3: May 27, 2026
- Round 4: June 3, 2026

Each round included Andes hantavirus PCR testing and serum IgM/IgG antibody testing. **All test results were negative.** The passenger remained in stable condition throughout and experienced no fever, cough, breathing difficulties, or any other symptoms suggestive of hantavirus infection.

**Outcome:**
Taiwan CDC confirmed that given the negative test results and the successful completion of the 42-day enhanced self-health management period, the passenger poses no risk of transmission to the community in Taiwan. Enhanced monitoring was officially lifted on June 7, 2026.

**International Notification:**
Taiwan's IHR National Focal Point notified both the World Health Organization (WHO) and the New Zealand IHR focal point of the outcome, consistent with IHR obligations.

**MV Hondius Outbreak Context:**
The broader MV Hondius cluster involved 13 total cases (11 confirmed, 2 probable) with 3 deaths across multiple nationalities.
