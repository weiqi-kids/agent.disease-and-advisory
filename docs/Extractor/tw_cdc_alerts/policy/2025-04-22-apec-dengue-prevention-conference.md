## 摘要
Taiwan CDC hosted an APEC-funded conference on April 22-23, 2025, bringing together international dengue experts to address prevention challenges in the post-COVID-19 era. The conference focused on innovative technologies, vector surveillance, and vaccine development against the backdrop of 2024's record dengue outbreak with over 14 million cases worldwide.

## 詳細內容

### Conference Overview
Taiwan CDC organized an APEC-funded conference on April 22-23 titled "Dengue Prevention and Control in the Post-COVID-19 Era: New Challenges and Role of Innovative Technology." The event brought together international dengue experts to strengthen regional prevention strategies.

### Context for the Conference
Dengue activity surged significantly in 2024. According to the announcement, "the number of dengue cases reached a record high, with a cumulative total of over 14 million reported infections and more than 10,000 related deaths worldwide." Contributing factors included lifted border restrictions and climate change impacts.

### Participants
High-level attendees included Taiwan's Deputy Minister of Health and Welfare, Taiwan CDC Director-General, Tainan City Mayor, and experts from multiple countries: the United States, Japan, Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, Indonesia, Mexico, and Vietnam. Academic and industry representatives also participated.

### Key Discussion Topics
The conference addressed:
- National dengue preparedness and response policies
- Vector surveillance and control methods
- Dengue vaccine development
- Innovative technology applications for vector monitoring

### Community Engagement Initiative
Taiwan CDC partnered with Tainan City Government to organize a field visit demonstrating practical dengue prevention through community-based mosquito breeding site elimination and environmental management improvements.
