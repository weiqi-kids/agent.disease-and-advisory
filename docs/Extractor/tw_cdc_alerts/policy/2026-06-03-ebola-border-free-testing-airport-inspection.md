## 摘要

Taiwan CDC announced on June 3, 2026, that free Ebola testing will be offered at four international airports from June 3 to June 30 for asymptomatic Taiwan nationals and inbound travelers with travel history to the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) or Uganda. Minister of Health and Welfare Shih Chung-liang personally inspected border quarantine operations at Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport as part of escalating response measures.

## 詳細內容

**Free Testing Program:**
- Period: June 3 – June 30, 2026
- Eligibility: Asymptomatic Taiwan nationals and inbound travelers with travel history to DRC or Uganda
- Testing locations:
  - Taipei Songshan Airport
  - Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport
  - Taichung International Airport
  - Kaohsiung International Airport

**Policy Measures:**
- Travel Health Notice raised to **Level 3: Warning** for both DRC and Uganda
- All travelers must report daily health status for **21 days** via the Self-monitoring Report System
- Violations subject to fines between NT$10,000–NT$150,000

**Ministerial Inspection:**
Minister Shih Chung-liang reviewed border quarantine operations at Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, overseeing the implementation of enhanced screening protocols and TOCC assessments for incoming international flights.

**Cross-Agency Coordination:**
The response involved multiple agencies including the National Immigration Agency, Civil Aviation Administration, and Taoyuan International Airport Corporation. Medical referral mechanisms were established at all ports of entry.

**Disease Background:**
The Ebola outbreak in DRC and Uganda was declared a PHEIC by WHO on May 17, 2026. The current outbreak is caused by Bundibugyo ebolavirus, for which no approved vaccine or specific treatment exists.
