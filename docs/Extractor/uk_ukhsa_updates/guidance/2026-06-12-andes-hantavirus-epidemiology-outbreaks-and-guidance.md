## 摘要

UKHSA 更新安地斯漢他病毒（ANDV）綜合指引，涵蓋流行病學、症狀、診斷與治療管理，並因 2026 年 5 月一艘郵輪爆發疫情而更新指引，將 ANDV 列為需要專科醫院管理的空氣傳播高危傳染病（HCID）。

## 詳細內容

The UK Health Security Agency updated comprehensive guidance on Andes hantavirus (ANDV), with the most recent revision in June 2026 following a cruise ship outbreak in May 2026.

**Epidemiology:**
- Andes virus (ANDV) is a New World Hantavirus, first identified in humans in 1995
- Endemic regions: four areas of Argentina (North, Centro, Northeast, Patagonia) and southern Chile (particularly Región de Los Lagos)
- Natural reservoir: long-tailed pygmy rice rat
- Seasonal peak: spring and summer when rodent populations are highest
- Travel-related cases have occurred in the US and Europe among returning travellers

**Key Feature — Human-to-Human Transmission:**
ANDV is unique among hantaviruses in exhibiting confirmed human-to-human transmission. This primarily occurs through close household contact, increasing infection risk tenfold in family settings. The May 2026 cruise ship outbreak raised concern about transmission in confined environments.

**Transmission Routes:**
- Primary: inhalation of aerosolised virus particles from infected rodent excreta (urine, droppings), or dust containing excreta
- Secondary (ANDV only): close person-to-person contact

**Clinical Features:**
- **Incubation period:** 9 to 40 days
- **Initial symptoms:** influenza-like illness — fever, muscle aches, gastrointestinal complaints
- **Progressive illness:** respiratory compromise, hypotension (Hantavirus Cardiopulmonary Syndrome — HCPS)
- **Mortality rate:** 21–50%
- Laboratory: thrombocytopenia (low platelets), elevated liver enzymes

**Management and Classification:**
UKHSA classifies ANDV as an **airborne High Consequence Infectious Disease (HCID)** requiring specialist hospital management.
