## 詳細內容

The Hepatitis C National Register is a patient data registry operated by the UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA) in London, supporting research into the natural history of hepatitis C virus (HCV) infection.

**Registry Overview:**
- Contains one of the largest cohorts of HCV patients in Europe with known infection acquisition dates
- Core population: transfusion recipients identified through the national HCV lookback programme
- Ongoing recruitment: paediatric cases and other patients with documented acquisition dates
- Data governance: multidisciplinary team guided by expert steering committee, operated by UKHSA Blood Safety, Hepatitis, STI and HIV Division

**Data Collection:**
- Standardised clinical report forms enabling systematic data gathering every 3–5 years
- Registry links clinical data with national mortality records and cancer registries
- Tracks disease progression for both cases and controls

**Research Focus Areas:**
1. Establishing the natural clinical trajectory of HCV infection
2. Identifying risk factors for progressive liver disease
3. Comparing infection patterns across different patient populations (age, gender, transmission route)
4. Long-term outcomes including cirrhosis, hepatocellular carcinoma, and all-cause mortality

**Most Recent Update:** 13 June 2026 (originally published 2 April 2013)

## 相關疾病

Hepatitis C virus (HCV), chronic liver disease, cirrhosis, hepatocellular carcinoma
