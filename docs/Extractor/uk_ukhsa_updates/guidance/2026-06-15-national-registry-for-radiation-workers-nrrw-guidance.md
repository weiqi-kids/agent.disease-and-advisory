## 摘要

UKHSA 發布英國輻射工作人員國家登記處（NRRW）指引，說明這個自 1976 年建立的研究資料庫如何監測超過 27 萬名核工業及國防工作者長期低劑量游離輻射的健康影響，並確認現行英國輻射防護措施提供充分的工作者保護。

## 詳細內容

The National Registry for Radiation Workers (NRRW) is a UK-based study and database established to monitor the health effects of long-term, low-dose occupational exposure to ionizing radiation.

**History and Purpose:**
- Established in 1976 by the National Radiological Protection Board, now operated by UKHSA
- Created because atomic bomb survivor data — the traditional basis for radiation risk estimates — may not apply to UK workers who receive lower doses over extended periods rather than single acute high-dose exposures
- Aims to investigate whether UK radiation protection regulations adequately safeguard workers' health

**Population Covered (270,000+ workers from):**
- Ministry of Defence
- Former British Nuclear Fuels Ltd sites
- UK Atomic Energy Authority
- UK nuclear power generation facilities
- Note: Healthcare sector workers are currently excluded

**Data Collection Process:**
Employers provide employment records, radiation exposure details, and personal identifiers. The system tracks workers across multiple employers to capture cumulative occupational doses. Follow-up data (mortality, cancer incidence) is obtained from NHS records and national cancer registries.

**Participation:**
- Operates on an opt-out basis with approximately 1% withdrawal rate
- Workers can withdraw by contacting radiationworkerepidemiology@UKHSA.gov.uk

**Key Findings:**
Results indicate current UK radiation protection measures provide adequate worker protection.
