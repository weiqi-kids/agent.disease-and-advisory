## 摘要

Illness and death due to Rocky Mountain spotted fever (RMSF) have been reported in the northern region of Mexico along the U.S. border, particularly in Baja California, Sonora, Chihuahua, Coahuila, and Nuevo León.

## 詳細內容

Illness and death due to Rocky Mountain spotted fever (RMSF) have been reported in the northern region of Mexico along the U.S. border, particularly in Baja California, Sonora, Chihuahua, Coahuila, and Nuevo León.

## 旅遊建議

**Level 1 - Practice Usual Precautions**: Travelers to northern Mexico should:
- Avoid contact with dogs that may have ticks
- Use EPA-registered insect repellents on exposed skin
- Treat clothing and gear with permethrin
- Check your body for ticks after outdoor activities
- Remove ticks promptly and properly if found
- Seek immediate medical care if you develop fever, headache, or rash after potential tick exposure
