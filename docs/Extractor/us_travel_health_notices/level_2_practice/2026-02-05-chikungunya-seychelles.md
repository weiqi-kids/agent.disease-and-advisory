# Level 2 - Chikungunya in Seychelles

**Date:** 2026-02-05
**Alert Level:** Level 2 - Practice Enhanced Precautions
**Destination:** Seychelles
**Disease:** Chikungunya
**Source:** [CDC Travel Health Notices](https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/notices/level2/chikungunya-seychelles)

## Summary

There is an outbreak of chikungunya in Seychelles. Travelers can protect themselves by preventing mosquito bites.

## Recommendations

- Practice enhanced precautions when traveling to Seychelles
- Prevent mosquito bites by using insect repellent
- Wear long-sleeved shirts and long pants
- Stay in places with air conditioning or window and door screens
- Use mosquito nets if sleeping outdoors or in rooms without screens

## About Chikungunya

Chikungunya is a viral disease transmitted to humans by infected mosquitoes. Symptoms typically include fever and joint pain, along with headache, muscle pain, joint swelling, or rash. Most people feel better within a week, but joint pain may persist for months.

## Additional Information

For more information about chikungunya and how to protect yourself, visit the CDC website or consult with a healthcare provider before travel.
